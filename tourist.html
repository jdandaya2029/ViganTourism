TOURIST SPOTS

Calle Crisologo

This place is the main center of Spanish architecture. This is surrounded by beautifully preserved ancestral houses. There are a lot of antique shops, jewelry stores, and cafes. It is one of the best heritage sites in the Philippines.

Plaza Salcedo

A beautiful plaza for wandering, it's a beautifully large square located near the Vigan Cathedral and known for its status of Juan de Salcedo. It's known for its large, modern dancing fountain that is a popular evening attraction. This site is also known where Gabriela Silang was executed in 1763.

Plaza Burgos

It was built to honor Father Jose P. Burgos, a native of Vigan who was martyred in 1872. The plaza is located next to St. Paul Cathedral. Today, Plaza Burgos serves as a cultural and social hub for both locals and visitors.

Vigan Cathedral (St. Paul’s Metropolitan Cathedral)

One of the most amazing historic sites in Vigan. It's founded by Juan de Salcedo, it was made of wood and stalk. It was restored in 1641 and its current baroque structure was finished in 1800. In 1758, it was established as a cathedral.

Bantay Bell Tower

One of the loveliest church towers in the city of Vigan. It was built in 1591 and converted to a church bell tower in 1857. It was used as a watchtower for invading enemy forces during World War ll.

Syquia Mansion Museum

This mansion is a stunning blend of Filipino, Chinese, and Spanish architectural influences. Inside you'll find a vast collection of artifacts, antique, furniture, and memorabilia of the Syquia family.

Padre Burgos House

It showcases the rich heritage of the Ilocos region and the Philippines. It features an extensive collection of artifacts, artworks, and historical treasures.

Pagburnayan Jar Factory

This place is where tourists can watch potters at work or join a hands-on experience to mold your own mini jar.

Bularte Zoo

It was established by Chavit Singson, he has a hunting hobby as his hunting trophy collection grew too big. He decided to open a zoo to promote conservation and protection of endangered species.

Crisologo Museum

It's the restored ancestral home of the Crisologo family. This museum is specifically focused to Congressman Floro S. Crisologo.

Archbishop's Palace

A historical Spanish-colonial building located next to the St. Paul Metropolitan Cathedral. It is the only remaining Archbishop’s Palace in the Philippines from that era.
